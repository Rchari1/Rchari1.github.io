Outreach and Diversity in Physics

The field of physics, while advancing rapidly, still faces significant challenges in diversity and inclusion. While it is hard to change the culture of a field overnight, as scientists we have the obligation to ensure the integrity, including the diversity and inclusivity, of the field we are in. Recognizing this, I helped found the "People of Color in Physics Organization" at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville. Our mission is to promote diversity, support underrepresented groups, and create opportunities for community engagement and mentorship.

The organization has grown to become a vital space for students and faculty, hosting discussions, workshops, and collaborative projects aimed at bridging the diversity gap in the physics community. We have also hosted multiple speakers with a focus on Physicists of Color to share their experiences and share advice on promoting diversity in the field. If you are interested in speaking please feel free to reach out!

With fellow executives of the People of Color in Physics Organization. Pictured from Left to Right; Cordney Nash (Outreach Coordinator), Cora Thomas (Vice President), Raghav Chari (President)

Our outreach efforts extend beyond the campus. A memorable event was our visit to the Boys and Girls Club in Knoxville, where we demonstrated the fun and intriguing aspects of physics through experiments like making liquid nitrogen ice cream. These activities not only bring joy to young minds but also inspire them to consider futures in science and technology.
